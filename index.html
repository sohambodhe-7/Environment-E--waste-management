Introduction

Electronic waste (E-waste) refers to discarded electrical and electronic devices such as computers, laptops, mobile phones, televisions, batteries, and home appliances. Due to rapid technological growth, e-waste is one of the fastest-growing waste streams globally. Improper disposal releases toxic substances like lead, mercury, and cadmium, harming both the environment and human health.

Sources of E-Waste

Household Waste

Old smartphones, televisions, washing machines, chargers, and kitchen electronics.

Industrial Waste

Manufacturing equipment, IT infrastructure, production devices, and machinery.

Commercial & Institutional

Offices, banks, colleges, hospitals, and organizations generating outdated devices.

Effects of E-Waste

Environmental Damage

Causes soil contamination, air pollution, and groundwater toxicity.

Health Hazards

Leads to respiratory illness, skin disorders, neurological damage, and cancer risk.

Resource Loss

Improper disposal wastes valuable metals like gold, silver, and copper.

E-Waste Recycling Process

Step 1: Collection from households, industries, and institutions.
Step 2: Segregation into categories for proper handling.
Step 3: Dismantling and manual separation of parts.
Step 4: Shredding into smaller pieces.
Step 5: Recovery of reusable metals and materials.

Solutions & Recommendations

Reduce

Purchase durable, eco-friendly, and energy-efficient products.

Reuse

Repair devices, donate, or resell usable electronics.

Recycle

Use certified recycling centers and support proper disposal systems.
