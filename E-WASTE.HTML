Introduction

E-waste refers to discarded electronic devices such as mobile phones, laptops, televisions, and batteries. Improper disposal causes pollution and health hazards due to toxic materials like lead, mercury, and cadmium.

Sources of E-Waste

Household Waste

Old phones, televisions, washing machines, chargers.

Industrial Waste

Manufacturing units, IT hardware, production sectors.

Commercial Waste

Banks, offices, schools, hospitals, institutions.

Effects of E-Waste

Environmental Impact

Soil contamination, water pollution, and air pollution.

Health Hazards

Respiratory issues, nervous system damage, skin diseases.

Economic Loss

Loss of reusable materials like gold, copper, and silver.

Solutions

Reduce

Reuse

Recycle

Use authorized recycling centers.
